With every major business segment having its help-line, tech-support or customer service number, call centers have become an integral part of commerce. A customer has taken for granted that he will be greeted with a friendly voice whenever he answers a marketing call or makes a call himself to the toll-free number of your company. But what goes on behind providing such seamless service is quite a story in itself.

When you call the toll-free number of your credit card provider, your call may be routed to another country thousands of kilometers away. The call you receive in your New York office from your credit card company informing you of special discounts may be from a person sitting in India. This is where the concept of call center service providers comes in. They may be serving a number of companies simultaneously. For example a call center service provider may be serving clients from multiple domains like banking, telecom service providers, internet service providers, computer vendors, health insurance companies, etc.

These call center service providers are called BPO’s (Business Process Outsourcing) and can offer numerous business process outsourcing solutions for any business. Some of them are like inbound telemarketing, outbound telemarketing, business to business (B2B) telemarketing, business to consumer (B2C) telemarketing, automated inbound and outbound IVR (Interactive Voice Response) telemarketing, telemarketing enhancement services, on-call technical and customer support, medical transcription and electronic data processing. They train their employees according to the process or project they will be working on. The in house Caucasian American trainers provide trainings that are exhaustive and cover areas like voice and accent, process, compliance and policies and principles.
